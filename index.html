Scribblings from the Walls of Eryx

by Bob from the North Country

What is this?

August 18, 2025

The sands of Venus are littered with crystals of immeasurable value. They appear to be for the taking. But intrepid explorers will soon discover that this is far from the case. On crystal-strewn plateaus, like Eryx, exist invisible walls organized in vast maze structures, which trap greedy crystal hunters in preparation for their slaughter by the native population of lizard creatures.

This is the premise of "In the Walls of Eryx" by H.P. Lovecraft and Kenneth Sterling. It is the source of one of this site's many titles. The tale is a Weird tale, with boundaries that do not conform to our understanding, which are authored instead by beings from beyond.

"Eryx" is one of Lovecraft's pulpier tales, but the central idea is the one animating all Weird literature; namely, that the ways we've come to cognize our world are insufficient, and that there might exist an order beyond our understanding. That's the best case scenario. The worst case scenario is that there's no order at all.

On this blog we will explore those tales and poems and essays and artifacts that explore this idea. It will be wide-ranging, from Beowulf to Cthulhu, from "The Second Coming" to The Tempest. We will explore the Weird--as mode, as genre, as mood, and as vibe...
